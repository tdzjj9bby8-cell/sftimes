    editor_note: |
      The 14,000-apartment loophole is the same statutory hole the Waldman case is testing, and it is now moving from anecdote to structural pattern with a public number. AB 1482, the state's 2019 rent cap, exempts buildings built within the last 15 years. Every year, some buildings age into coverage and their tenants gain protection. Every year, landlords holding buildings still inside the exemption window have a shrinking runway to reset rents to market. The Gantry displacement is the visible case. The 14,000-unit figure is the exposure. Sacramento can close the loophole by cutting the exemption window or removing it, but the CBRE and NAHB lobbying that killed the last attempt has not weakened. The realistic local fix is the constructive-eviction litigation strategy the Tenants Union is pursuing case by case.
