## 01 · Green Apple Books
**Inner Richmond · 506 Clement Street**

The flagship. Three stories. The fiction floor is the largest used selection in the city. The basement annex (behind the staircase, easy to miss) has rare poetry and the only complete used set of the New York Review of Books classics anyone keeps in stock. Staff knows the inventory. Open until 10:30 wed through sat. Cash gets a 10% trade-in bonus on books you bring in.

## 02 · Adobe Books
**Mission · 3130 24th Street**

Cooperative-owned since 2013, after the Valencia closure. Smaller than Green Apple but more curated. The art and small-press fiction sections are unmatched in the city. They host readings monthly. The owner, Andrew, will hold a book for two weeks if you call. They take credit cards but prefer cash. Closed Tuesdays.

## 03 · Dog Eared Books
**Mission · 900 Valencia Street**

Has been on Valencia since 1992. The corner location is the original. They have a second location in Castro and a third in the Mission's outer reach. The Valencia store is the one to visit. The non-fiction is deep. The poetry is real. They will give you a quote on a box of books in under ten minutes. Open daily until 10pm.

## 04 · Alley Cat Books
**Mission · 3036 24th Street**

Two blocks from Adobe. They run a separate gallery in the back. The used selection is heavier on art, photography, and design than other shops. They have a back patio with three tables where you can read what you buy. The owner, Marie, will let you check a book out for an afternoon if you leave your ID. Closed Mondays. Tuesday is poetry night.

## 05 · The Booksmith
**Haight · 1644 Haight Street**

Mixed new and used, but the used section in the back is the under-recognized one. They have the best used cookbook selection in the city, including a working set of the original Time-Life Foods of the World series that someone keeps trying to buy and they keep refusing to sell. Strong on travel and local history. Open daily.
