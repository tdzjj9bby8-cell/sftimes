# SF Times Exhibition · UX/UI/Design/Content Audit
## Date: 2026-05-31
## Scope
`src/pages/exhibition.astro`, all of `src/pages/exhibition/`, `ExhibitionLayout.astro`, `ExhibitionMasthead.astro`, `Placeholder.astro`. Read-only pass. No code changed. No deploy.

## Reference scan

References drawn from training knowledge of the public sites listed. Patterns to steal and patterns to avoid for each.

### hellgatenyc.com
Closest model match: worker-owned, subscriber-funded, hyperlocal.
- **Steal:** named reporter is always above the headline. Their bylines are bigger than ours, and they treat reporters as a brand. We currently hide author names from homepage cards.
- **Avoid:** their category chips ("News," "Food," "Civic") clutter the header and read taxonomic. We do not have category chips and should not add them.

### defector.com
Reader-owned, no ads, opinion-led.
- **Steal:** photographer credit is paired with the photo in a small caps line directly under the image, not buried in the byline. Universal, not conditional.
- **Avoid:** their dark theme on long reads is fatiguing. Our cream paper is more humane; do not regress to black backgrounds for body text.

### atlasobscura.com
Profile-driven discovery, image-first.
- **Steal:** map embed on profiles ties a place to a coordinate. We could do a "Map of Keepers" view that pins every featured subject to a neighborhood.
- **Avoid:** sponsored content cards interleaved into the editorial grid. We have a five-principles wall against this; keep it.

### magazine.atavist.com
Long-form, scroll-driven, near-zero nav.
- **Steal:** the article page strips chrome aggressively. Tiny utility, no section nav, just the story. Our story page still carries the full ExhibitionLayout including section nav and newsletter; could be lighter inside the article.
- **Avoid:** their landing pages don't curate. Just a wall of features. Our asymmetric grid is more opinionated; keep that, don't go flat.

### punchdrink.com
Profile structure (subject + place + craft), photographer credits prominent.
- **Steal:** profile pages lead with a photographer credit in the eyebrow itself, before the headline. Reinforces "this is craft." We push it into the byline as a conditional.
- **Avoid:** their archive page filters by drink category, which scales poorly. Our `/exhibition/stories` is unfiltered too but it's only 57 cards; the better fix is sort + neighborhood tags, not categories.

### thedriftmag.com
Literary mag, signature hyperlinked-paragraph landing.
- **Steal:** their homepage is a single editor's paragraph with every issue title hyperlinked inline. Radical, text-first, distinctively literary. We could replace OR pair with the asymmetric grid; would be a real franchise move.
- **Avoid:** their issue archive is a chronological wall with no visual rhythm. Reads like a list. Our archive strip is better.

### Bonus references
- **404media.co** — named reporters as a brand, donor wall on about page. Their feature wall is too blog-roll; ours is better.
- **missionlocal.org** — hyperlocal SF nonprofit. Donate-now CTA persistent in nav. Their typography is dated; ours is opinionated. Their persistent donate CTA is the model we're missing.

## Prioritized punch list

### P0 — blocking
1. **Fix cross-tree link from exhibition/support to legacy /support.**
   `src/pages/exhibition/support.astro:48` → change `/support` to `/exhibition/support` or remove the colmark.
   Why: a reader bouncing through the "Where the Money Goes" CTA lands in the legacy keepers design system. Disorienting. Hellgate keeps cross-section links within one design system.

2. **Self-host Anton and Inter Tight.**
   `src/layouts/ExhibitionLayout.astro:28-29` → download woff2 to `public/fonts/`, replace the CDN `<link>` tags with `@font-face` declarations.
   Why: third-party CDN dependency, slower LCP, no `font-display: swap` control. Legacy keepers fonts are already self-hosted; exhibition should match.

3. **Remove em-dash from legacy `Header.astro:21` aria-label.**
   `src/components/Header.astro:21` → change `aria-label="the keepers. — SF Times"` to `aria-label="the keepers. SF Times"`.
   Why: Eric's rule is zero em-dashes anywhere, including screen-reader copy. Ships in legacy production today.

4. **Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` on the homepage carousel.**
   `src/pages/exhibition.astro:317` → wrap `start()` in a `matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)').matches` check and skip auto-rotate.
   Why: WCAG 2.2 (2.3.3 Animation from Interactions). Reduced-motion users see a constantly shifting hero with no opt-out.

5. **Remove false sort promise on `/exhibition/stories`.**
   `src/pages/exhibition/stories.astro:27` → either build the sort UI or change the line.
   Why: "Sortable by neighborhood and beat" is currently a lie. Trust violation.

6. **Scrub the kitchen-sink template of typographic dashes and ASCII arrows.**
   `src/pages/exhibition/template.astro:152` → replace `— [ATTRIBUTION], [CONTEXT] →` with hyphen-only or comma-only equivalents.
   Why: anyone copying this block inherits the em-dash. Template should be the safest place in the repo.
